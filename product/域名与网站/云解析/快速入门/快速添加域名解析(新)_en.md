### Add a Record
1. Logging in to [Cloud DNS Console](https://console.cloud.tencent.com/domain/cns) and click **Add Resolution**. 
2. Entering the domain name to be resolved and click **OK**.
![1](//mc.qcloudimg.com/static/img/8231080d9d713baf5d4edf4163b23ee0/image.png)
![2](//mc.qcloudimg.com/static/img/f55d8b825625d9520bd898786015eee5/image.png)
3. Clicking **Resolution** for the added domain name.
![5](//mc.qcloudimg.com/static/img/23cc5f386b2e71663945278356aaba2a/image.png)
4. On the domain name record management page, click **Add Record** and add the record to be resolved.
![6](//mc.qcloudimg.com/static/img/9bc87c2df574e7eff063be5e01c9f651/image.png)
![7](//mc.qcloudimg.com/static/img/8bbed2eba078411a354b28bd967bd58c/image.png)
### Modify Domain Name DNS Address
For more information, please see [Change DNS Server](https://cloud.tencent.com/document/product/302/5518).
### Wait for Resolution to Take Effect
After completing these two steps, wait until the resolution takes effect.
>**Note:**
>A modified DNS server needs 0-72 hours to take effect globally. The record may not work for certain regions if 72 hours haven't elapsed.
